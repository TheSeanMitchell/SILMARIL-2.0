# SILMARIL — The Narrative (Alpha 0.001)

*A starter narrative. The story of what this is, where it's been, and what it's for.
Keep it honest; update it as the truth changes.*

---

## The itch

This project started from an itch that won't go away: the suspicion that somewhere
in the noise of the stock market there is a **repeatable edge** — and that a system
of many small, opinionated minds, arguing with each other and keeping score, might
find it where a single strategy couldn't.

So SILMARIL was built as a kind of parliament of traders. Dozens of agents, each
with a temperament — some cautious, some aggressive, some contrarian, some trend-
followers — all looking at the same stocks and reaching their own verdicts. A senate
to arbitrate them. A scoreboard to judge them. A learning loop to promote the ones
that were right and quiet the ones that weren't. The dream was a machine that would
**trade, burn through data, and learn — and learn, and learn** — until it got good.

## The drift

Along the way it grew. It grew a crypto compounder, a token trader, a sports-betting
bot. It grew a beautiful dashboard with dozens of panels. It grew a senate that was
supposed to breed brand-new agents like Pokémon. It grew clever.

What it didn't grow was an edge. Worse, a lot of the cleverness turned out to be
hollow on inspection: the learning loop had been training on stale prices and
concluded that the agents who did nothing were the best ones. The fancy execution
path was quietly getting its orders rejected. The breeder only ever wrote proposals
for offspring that never traded. The compounders were inventing money. And under all
of it, the real accounts were drifting along *behind* a simple index fund.

A month of aggravation. The honest diagnosis: not a fake system — a *real* one, doing
a great deal of genuine work, pointed slightly wrong and running on bad data.

## The rebirth

Alpha 0.001 is the reset. Not a reset of ambition — a reset of focus.

We strip away everything that isn't the itch. No crypto, no tokens, no sports — they
were never the point, and they couldn't even trade on the broker. What's left is the
thing we actually wanted from the beginning: **real agents, trading real stocks,
producing real results, judged honestly against the market.**

The data is clean now. Execution works. The learning loop has teeth on the agents it
trains. For the first time, the next week of data will actually *mean* something.

## What we're building toward

A machine that earns the right to each of its parts. Where an analytic only survives
if it changes a trade for the better. Where "learning" means the agents that make
money get more capital, measured in dollars and not in flattering win-rates. Where
the agents don't just get promoted and demoted but genuinely *evolve* — their
parameters mutating toward what works. Where the front page is a single honest number:
**are we beating the market yet — or not.**

If the answer turns out to be "not" — if the edge isn't here — the machine will say
so, plainly, and we'll have learned something real. That's the deal. No stories.

But the itch says it's here somewhere. So we trade, we burn data, we learn, we train.
Again, and again, and again.
